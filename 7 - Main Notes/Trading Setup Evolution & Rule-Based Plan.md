# Trading Setup Evolution & Rule-Based Plan

## Part 1: Setup Evolution (May 2024 → Feb 2026)

### Phase 1: Foundation & ICT Basics (May–Jul 2024)
**Charts reviewed:** 6 May, 13 May, 31 May, 18 Jul, 25 Jul

Earliest charts show the building blocks being learned. Setups referenced during this period:

- **Order blocks** (bullish/bearish) — marked with red/green shaded zones
- **FVGs (Fair Value Gaps)** — identified but not yet the primary entry mechanism
- **BSL/SSL (Buy-Side/Sell-Side Liquidity)** — marked on nearly every chart
- **Bullish/Bearish Market Structure** — annotated as "Bullish Market Structure," "Snap Back"
- **Stop hunts** — noted as "Took out $BSL relative equal" style annotations
- **ICT references** — one chart explicitly labelled "_ICT" suggesting study from ICT methodology

**Key observation:** Trade annotations during this phase show a wider range of entry reasons. The 13 May chart notes a trade entry based on order blocks with a lengthy self-reflection note. The 18 Jul chart shows 3 trades in one day — including an explicitly labelled "Revenge traded" Trade 2 after Trade 1 was a loss. The 31 May chart references "Low Probability Conditions" with a checklist (weekly objective met, TGIF objective met, inventory correction). R:R calculations are present (e.g., "Risk/Reward Ratio = 8.72") but entries are more exploratory.

**Setups used:** Order blocks, FVGs, liquidity sweeps. No consistent macro timing framework yet.

### Phase 2: Refining the ICT Framework (Aug–Oct 2024)
**Charts reviewed:** 20 Aug, 6 Sep Journal, 25 Sep Journal, 3 Oct Journal, 7 Oct Journal, 21 Oct Journal

This is a major inflection point. The annotation style becomes far more structured:

- **Volume Imbalance** — the 20 Aug chart prominently references "1D Volume Imbalance" and "15 Volume Imbalance" as directional bias tools
- **FVG validation as iFVG** — the concept of FVGs becoming "invalidated" (iFVG) emerges as a directional signal. The 6 Sep Journal chart shows this explicitly: "f-FVG created and breached a breakaway gap, showing that price wants to go lower"
- **Opening Day Range (ODR)** — highlighted in cyan, becomes a standard annotation
- **Bearish Breaker blocks** — the 7 Oct Journal shows a clear bearish breaker entry with partials: "4 contracts 47 handles, 1 contract 65.25 handles"
- **Displacement candles** — referenced on 20 Aug as "displacement candle. Perfect price action"
- **Relative equal highs/lows** — used as liquidity targets
- **Market structure shift** — explicitly identified and used as a confirmation signal
- **Silver Bullet (AM/PM)** — appears for the first time around Sep-Oct

**Key observation:** The 6 Sep chart is the most detailed annotation from 2024. It includes: economic calendar awareness, f-FVG validation, bearish order block identification, sell-side liquidity run, and a detailed optional trade setup. This is where the methodology crystallises. The 25 Sep chart shows ODR measurement ("75 handles"), breakaway gaps, and the first reference to reading how 15m candles validate or invalidate PD arrays.

**Setups used:** FVG/iFVG validation, bearish/bullish breakers, order blocks, market structure shifts, Silver Bullet timing. Beginning of a "wait and confirm" approach.

### Phase 3: Session Awareness & Macro Timing (Nov–Dec 2024)
**Charts reviewed:** 5 Nov, 20 Nov, 10 Dec, 6 Dec (27 Dec)

The chart layout changes significantly. Session labels now appear on every chart:

- **"Opening Range," "AM Silver Bullet," "Lunch," "1330 Macro," "PM Silver Bullet"** — marked as vertical zones
- **Previous Day PM high/low** — become standard reference levels (e.g., "191124 PM low," "191124 AM high")
- **London Session high/low** — explicitly marked as levels
- **Midnight Opening Range** — referenced on the Jan 2025 charts, likely introduced here
- **AM low / AM high** — used as structure levels for entries
- **f-FVG as entry after AM Silver Bullet** — the 20 Nov chart shows "Trade 1: AM Silver Bullet. Price already took out the 191124 AM low and resulted in a simple structure shift... Terminus was reached in 14 minutes."

**Key observation:** The 10 Dec chart has a full pre-session write-up on the left side: "Bearish bias," previous day PM levels, London session levels, targets, and considerations ("NFP Week. No trades. Only watch"). This is the emergence of the structured pre-session routine. Also: "CPI, post NFP week, Monday saw a dump and retrace on election results." Macro awareness is now deeply integrated.

**Setups used:** AM Silver Bullet entries, f-FVG with session confirmation, previous day PM structure, London session sweeps. Macro timing (AM/PM Silver Bullet, 1330 Macro) becomes central.

## Part 3: Rule-Based Trading Plan

### Pre-Session Checklist (Complete BEFORE 0700)

**Step 1: Calendar & Context Filter**

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| NFP week | No trading. Tape read only. |
| CPI / PPI day | No trading. Tape read only. |
| Bank holiday | No trading. Tape read only. |
| Previous day range > 200 handles | Caution. Expect high resistance. Tape read unless setup is textbook. |
| US Government shutdown active | Trade with caution. Reduce size or sit out if combined with no news driver. |
| No news driver + any other headwind | No trading. High resistance likely. |

If 2+ caution conditions are active → **tape read only, no trades.**

**Step 2: Daily Chart Bias (1D)**

1. Note the previous day's candle: body close relative to wick. Where did the body close relative to the previous candle?
2. Identify the nearest 1D BISI/SIBI and their quadrant levels (0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0).
3. Are there any daily wick levels that PD arrays are layered above? If yes, these are the highest-conviction levels for the day.
4. Mark the previous day's PM high, PM low, AM high, AM low.

**Step 3: 15m Chart Narrative (Pre-Market)**

1. Mark all relevant 15m BISI and SIBI on the chart.
2. Identify which 15m PD arrays price is currently respecting (bodies above/below C.E).
3. Calculate the ODR. If ODR < 50 handles → expect lackluster/high resistance day. Lower conviction.
4. Note the London session high and low (0200-0500).
5. Determine bias: Are 15m candle bodies adhering to a BISI (bullish) or SIBI (bearish)?

---

### Premarket Session (0650-0710 Macro)

**Entry Rules:**

1. Identify any smooth highs or lows swept during Asia session (1800-0500).
2. Observe what London killzone (0200-0500) did to those levels.
3. Wait for an f-FVG to form between 0700-0900.
4. Confirm the f-FVG aligns with the 15m narrative:
   - If 15m bias is bullish → look for f-FVG validated as BISI (bodies above upper quadrant)
   - If 15m bias is bearish → look for f-FVG validated as iFVG/SIBI
5. **Entry:** At the C.E of the f-FVG (or the relevant quadrant level if a PD array sits at a wick quadrant).
6. **Stop loss:** Below the f-FVG low (for longs) or above the f-FVG high (for shorts). Should be 3.5-16.75 handles based on historical range.
7. **Terminus:** The nearest BSL/SSL or 15m PD array. Target 20 handles minimum, 40 handles stretch.

**Confirmation signals (need at least 2):**

- [ ] Breakaway gap present
- [ ] Bodies of price adhering to upper quadrant (for longs) or lower quadrant (for shorts)
- [ ] 15m candle has validated the PD array in the direction of the trade
- [ ] Macro timing window (0650-0710)
- [ ] PD array sits at a daily wick quadrant level

---

### AM Session (0950-1010 Macro)

**Entry Rules:**

1. Observe the first 15m candle (0930-0944). Did it validate or invalidate any 15m PD arrays?
2. Wait for the second 15m candle (0945-0959) to confirm. Two consecutive candles in the same direction = strong signal.
3. Look for the 1m f-FVG formed during the opening range.
4. Determine if the f-FVG is validated as BISI (bullish) or iFVG (bearish):
   - **Bullish setup:** f-FVG holds as BISI → entry at f-FVG C.E (or 15m PD array C.E if they coincide)
   - **Bearish setup:** f-FVG validated as iFVG → wait for a 1m bearish breaker or SIBI for short entry

**Entry types (in order of preference):**

| Priority | Setup | Entry | Stop Loss |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | f-FVG at 15m PD array + wick quadrant | C.E of the 15m PD array | Below/above the 15m PD array |
| 2 | f-FVG C.E | C.E of the f-FVG | Below/above f-FVG low/high |
| 3 | 1m Bearish/Bullish Breaker | C.E of the breaker | Above/below breaker high/low |
| 4 | 1m BISI/SIBI at quadrant | Upper/lower quadrant of PD array | Below/above PD array |

**Do NOT enter if:**

- f-FVG is too far from current price (price has already left the zone)
- 15m candles are overlapping/strangling (high resistance)
- ODR < 50 handles and no breakaway gap
- Bodies of price are not adhering to a clear quadrant level
- It's past 1010 and no setup has formed (wait for 1050-1110 macro instead)

---

### Trade Management Rules

**Partial-Taking (4 contracts):**

| Contracts | Target | When |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | First target: nearest minor BSL/SSL or 15m PD array high/low | As soon as first target is reached |
| 1 | Second target: Terminus (ODR C.E, or further 15m PD array) | Hold until reached |
| 1 | Runner: next BSL/SSL beyond Terminus | Move stop to breakeven after partial 1. Let it run. |

**Stop Loss Movement:**

1. After first partial → move stop loss to entry (breakeven).
2. After price creates a new 1m BISI/SIBI in your direction → move stop to that PD array's C.E.
3. If price reaches Terminus → close position entirely unless the runner is at breakeven.

**Max Daily Loss: 15 handles.** If hit, stop trading for the day. No exceptions.

**Max Trades Per Day: 2.** If both lose, tape read the rest of the session.

---

### High Resistance Checklist

Price is high resistance if 3+ of these are true:

- [ ] 15m candles are overlapping with large wicks in both directions
- [ ] f-FVG is being violated repeatedly (bodies crossing in and out)
- [ ] ODR < 50 handles
- [ ] PD arrays are not being respected (price passes through without displacement)
- [ ] No breakaway gap after the first 15 minutes
- [ ] Multiple minor highs/lows being taken without follow-through (strangling)

**If high resistance → close any open positions and stop trading. Tape read only.**

---

### No-Trade Rules (Absolute)

1. **Never trade NFP week** (Mon-Fri of the week NFP is released). Especially if Monday is a bank holiday.
2. **Never trade CPI/PPI release day.** Tape read only.
3. **Never chase price.** If the entry level is missed by more than 2 handles, let it go.
4. **Never revenge trade.** If Trade 1 is a loss, Trade 2 must meet ALL entry criteria independently. If not, walk away.
5. **Never trade TGIF** unless premarket setup is textbook and completed before 0930.
6. **Never enter without 15m confirmation.** The 1m chart is for execution only. The 15m chart dictates the bias.
7. **Never enter outside macro timing windows** (0650-0710 premarket, 0950-1010 AM, 1050-1110 secondary AM).
