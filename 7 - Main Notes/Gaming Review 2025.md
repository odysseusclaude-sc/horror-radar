## Introduction
What a beautiful year it has been for gaming! **1,091 beautiful hours** poured into games, marking a **51% increase** from last year. What changed? I'm not sure either! Note that Live Service games like _Marvel Rivals_, _Infinity Nikki_, _Monster Hunter Wilds_, _Helldivers 2_, _Overwatch 2_, _Battlefield 6_, and _Where Winds Meet_ are marked as "Completed" as long as I played them for **more than 10 hours**. 

Why did I start tracking my gaming sessions? I wanted to see how I was spending my time and how much of it was spent on gaming. Based on assumptions, there are around 261 working days in 2025, with 9 of them being Public Holidays. I also had 10 days of reservist and I took 12 days of leave, that leaves me with 230 working days. Including my commute to office, assuming I went office everyday, I spent around **2,185** hours at my full time job. 

Assuming 365 days a year, I have a total of **8,760** hours in 2025. Almost **25%** of it was spent on gaming, **13%** spent on gaming, another **30%** spent on sleeping (assuming 7 hours per night), what did I do with the remaining **2,896 hours**? 

8,760 - 2,185 (work) - 1,124 (gaming) - (365x7 = 2,555) (sleeping) = 2,896 hours (~33%)

### Data Collection
Since 2024, I have manually tracked every gaming session on a Excel file. Be it while I'm gaming on my PC or the Steam Deck, I log every session. Manually. Well, it honestly doesn't take a lot of effort since I'm just logging the date and time. The price is also manually compiled on my end, whenever I make a purchase on Steam. On to some number crunching.

## Gaming Habits Analysis
For this analysis, I am going to exclude Marvel Rivals, because it is logged at 329 hours, almost 30% of my total game time. Also, I mainly played Marvel Rivals with friends and I am trying to analyze my own gaming habits.

### Monthly Trend with Marvel Rivals
Across the months, Jan is definitely an outlier, more than twice the monthly average of around 91 hours played per month. Jun, Jul and Aug also managed to reach 3 digit hours and I'm pretty sure it's because I got to work from home then. The office was running out of space so we were in between transitioning to the new office. Well, obviously, that was a glorious time. Looking at the total number of hours played, we can see that the bulk of my gaming was done on the weekends (that's a given), Friday then Wednesday. Across all the months, Tuesday has the least number of hours played, sometimes I don't even play on Tuesdays at all. That's because I have Korean lesson with my friend after work and we usually grab dinner after. On a monthly trend, the bulk of my gaming was done in Jan and in the middle of the year. 

### Month Trend without Marvel Rivals
There are some interesting tidbits here. Even though I spent almost 100+ hours playing Marvel Rivals, I still managed to find time to squeeze in another 90+ hours of gaming. From Thursday onwards, we can see that my gaming habits picked up. Interestingly, we can see that the bulk of my gaming was done on a Thursday. 

By comparing Feb and Mar, we can see that I played almost twice as many hours in Mar but I logged lesser sessions than expected. This means that each session in Mar was a lot longer, which we can see in the Average Hours Played. Interestingly, Mar is also the month with the highest average hours played. And why is that? I spent the entire month playing **MH Wilds**. And then in April, the number of total hours drop drastically even though the number of session were almost the same. 

May was reservist so i was gone for more than 2 weeks, along with needing to catch up on work when I came back. It was a very sad month for gaming. I started to pick things up again in Jun with Clair Obscur: Expedition 33, and that led to a long run of RPG games. 

If you remember the monthly visual from before, you would have seen that Jan was the month with the most number of hours played. After removing Marvel Rivals, we can see that my own gaming habits peaked in Aug, with a whopping 124.8 hours, and I didn't even game on Tuesdays. More on August later in the Month by Month Section.

In the later months, we can see that I am averaging almost 70 hours per month. Obviously, gaming picks up again in Dec and less time was spent at work.
