## Final Draft
After 17.43 hours, I finally finished my first playthrough of Silent Hill f. I'm glad I wrapped it up in time for the October spooky season and the launch of Battlefield 6. The game has 4 main endings, which will require a few more playthroughs, but Battlefield 6 is out today so...

This was my first Silent Hill game, so I wasn't sure what to expect so I decided to play on Hard mode. It wasn't as difficult as I thought, mainly because I was pretty diligent about scavenging and saving my supplies, so I never ran out of anything. The combat was surprisingly fun, and I think the great audio design is a big reason why. The sound when a heavy hit or a counterattack connects is just really satisfying.

Of course, the main draw here is the horror. The atmosphere of 1960s Japan is unsettling, eerie, and beautiful all at once. There's a certain vibe to Japanese horror that's just different and gets under your skin. The game looks great, but my only real issue was with the camera work. It felt a bit janky, and in the many narrow alleyways, it could feel pretty claustrophobic. The whole experience is elevated by the sound design—the traditional drum beats and weird singing are incredibly effective at making you, the player, want to get out of there. Overall, I really enjoyed this and will come back to it to unlock the other endings.
