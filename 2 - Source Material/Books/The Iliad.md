Epic: _an expansive poem of enormous scope, composed in an old-fashioned and superbly elevated style of language, concerning the wondrous deeds of heroes_.

Heroes were mortal, endowed with superhuman abilities and descended from the immortal gods themselves. And in The Iliad, we have Achilles.

Themes: Mortality, Self Destruction

Mortality: All the ordeals of the human condition culminate in the ultimate ordeal of a warrior hero's violent death in battle, detailed in all its ghastly varieties in The Iliad.

Achilles, a man who chooses violent death over life in order to win the glory of being remembered forever in epic poetry. After Patroclus' death, the anger of Achilles was worded in such a way that even rivals how it was worded for Zeus. Even more when Achilles was ready to fight the Trojans, Zeus allows the deathless gods to join the war freely so that the Trojans will not be immediately wiped out, showing how much power Achilles had. Achilles is initially angry with Agamemnon after he insulted and disgraced Achilles' honour by taking away Brises, a lady that was bestowed to him after winning a war.
